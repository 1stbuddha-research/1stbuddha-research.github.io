Purpose of Microscopic Examination

Microscopy reveals the microstructure of authentic Nepal bronze Buddha sculptures. Newar metalworkers left distinctive tool marks, engraving strokes and granular gold traces embedded deep within chased ornament. These features cannot be reproduced by modern casting.

The images on this page document:

• Remains of original fire-gilded 24 kt gold
• Sindoor and carbon-based pigment residues
• Cuprite and malachite crystallisation
• Natural oxidation and aged surface coatings
• Chisel cuts and hand-engraved robe patterns

All microscope images relate to Nepal bronze objects analysed through the same UV–microscopy–internal structure method described across this catalogue.
