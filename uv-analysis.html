Why UV-Light Matters

UV-light inspection is one of the most effective non-destructive tools for distinguishing authentic Nepal bronze sculptures from later workshop production or modern replicas.

Early Newar and Himalayan bronzes show specific UV behaviours:

• Weak, matte fluorescence from natural varnish oxidisation
• Deep-green reaction in old malachite deposits
• Orange–brown cuprite response in gilding recesses
• No “plastic” or synthetic coating shine
• Broken, irregular fluorescence consistent with aged fire gilding

Modern bronzes typically show uniform bright-blue fluorescence from synthetic lacquers or chemically applied patina layers. None of these appear on the documented Nepal Buddha pieces.
