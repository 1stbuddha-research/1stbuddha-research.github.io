How UV, Microscopy & Casting Evidence Identify Authentic Nepal Bronzes

1. UV-Light Analysis

UV light reveals old gilding residues, natural mineral fluorescence and surface coatings. Genuine 17th-century Nepal bronzes show granular gold response, scattered malachite crystals and stable cuprite patterns that cannot be faked with modern patina chemicals.

2. Microscopy

Microscopy exposes Newar hand-chasing, tool marks, oxidation layers and centuries-old wear. The robe engraving of early bronzes shows deep, confident strokes, while later workshop pieces display shallow or repeated mechanical patterns.

3. Internal Casting Structures

Endoscopic inspection of the inner cavity reveals core material, casting joins, burnt clay residues and mineral accretions. Authentic bronzes display uneven walls, casting ribs, and natural oxidation that develops slowly over centuries.

4. Dating Through Technical Evidence

Material behaviour, gilding texture, corrosion mineralogy, and casting complexity allow reliable dating into periods such as the 16th–17th century Newar tradition. These markers are far more dependable than style alone.
