About This Catalogue

This repository presents a museum-style catalogue of authentic Nepalese bronze sculpture. Each object is documented through microscopy, UV-light inspection, alloy study, and internal structural analysis. The objective is to provide transparent, research-grade documentation of Himalayan fire-gilded bronzes and the Newar metalworking tradition that shaped the artistic landscape of Nepal, Tibet, Mustang and the Western Himalayas.

Featured Nepal Bronze: Buddha (59 cm)

This monumental Nepalese bronze exemplifies 17th-century Newar craftsmanship: complex multi-part casting, preserved mercury gilding, and deep hand-cut chasing across the robe. Microscopy confirms granular gold residue, natural cuprite–malachite formation, and centuries-old structural oxidation inside the cavity. Documentation continues in the linked sub-pages.

Objectives of the Research

• Identify and document authentic Nepal bronze sculpture through technical evidence.
• Provide high-resolution microscopy and UV-light material studies.
• Compare casting signatures with museum collections (Patan Museum, Rubin Museum, LACMA).
• Build an accessible reference archive for scholars, collectors and institutions.
• Support transparent valuation through structural, metallurgical and art-historical analysis.
